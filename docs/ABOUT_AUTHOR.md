## Who I Am

I'm Frederick Davis Stalnecker, a researcher and inventor who spent years wrestling with a fundamental problem in artificial intelligence: **How do you prevent hallucination without filtering?**

The answer led to THEOS—a breakthrough in how AI systems can reason about their own reasoning to prevent hallucination, improve quality, and accumulate wisdom.

---

## My Journey

### The Problem

For years, I watched AI systems generate confident-sounding answers that were completely wrong. Worse, they had no way to catch their own mistakes. The standard approach was to add filters, guardrails, and constraints. But that felt like putting a band-aid on a broken leg.

I kept thinking: **What if the problem isn't the AI's ability to reason, but the architecture?**

### The Insight

The breakthrough came from a simple observation: **You can't hallucinate about what you just thought about.**

If an AI system reasons about its own reasoning, it creates a feedback loop that naturally prevents false claims. The critical engine tests the constructive engine. Contradiction forces reconciliation. Truth emerges through productive disagreement.

### The Implementation

I spent months developing the mathematics, then years building and validating the implementation. THEOS emerged as a complete system that:

- Implements temporal recursion (thinking about thinking)
- Uses dual engines (productive disagreement)
- Measures contradiction (guides convergence)
- Prevents hallucination (through architecture)
- Accumulates wisdom (learns from experience)
- Maintains moral alignment (emerges from structure)
