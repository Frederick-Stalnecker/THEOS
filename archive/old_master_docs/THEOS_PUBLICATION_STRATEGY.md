## ADDRESSING LIKELY CRITICISMS

### Criticism 1: "This is just ensemble reasoning"
**Response:**
- Ensembles average predictions; THEOS preserves and synthesizes contradictions
- THEOS has formal convergence guarantees; ensembles don't
- THEOS learns from contradictions (wisdom accumulation); ensembles don't

### Criticism 2: "This adds compute overhead"
**Response:**
- Yes, THEOS runs two reasoning paths (overhead)
- But it catches errors that single-pass reasoning misses (benefit)
- Trade-off is justified for high-stakes decisions
- Efficiency improvements shown in benchmarks (15-35% savings in some cases)

### Criticism 3: "This doesn't work for all domains"
**Response:**
- Correct. THEOS is designed for domains where contradiction matters
- Not needed for simple classification tasks
- Essential for high-stakes reasoning (medical, legal, financial, scientific)
- Theological application shows domain-agnostic nature of framework
